7. Key Insights & Tactical Recommendations

Insights:

  • Conversion Drop: The slight dip in conversion rate from 3.5% to 3.2% suggests possible friction during checkout. This warrants a detailed UX review.
  • Rising Ad Costs: Increases in CPM and CPC in certain channels (notably Paid Social) indicate intensifying competition.
  • Subscriber Churn: Although new subscriber numbers are strong, the 4.5% churn rate is higher than desired. Investigate cancellation feedback and consider targeted retention campaigns.
  • Cohort Performance: Healthy repurchase rates and LTV in cohorts are promising; however, strategies to further drive repeat purchases should be explored.

Recommendations:

  • Optimize Checkout Flow: Initiate A/B testing for a simplified checkout process to reduce friction and potentially lift the conversion rate.
  • Review Ad Strategies: Reassess creative assets and bidding strategies for Paid Social to address the rising CPM and CPC.
  • Enhance Retention Efforts: Launch a targeted campaign to understand and reduce subscriber churn, possibly through personalized offers or feedback surveys.
  • Leverage Successful Campaigns: Scale up high-performing campaigns (e.g., the Paid Search channel and the creative that boosted click-through rates) to maximize overall efficiency.
  • Cohort Engagement: Develop initiatives to further encourage repeat purchases within strong-performing cohorts, such as loyalty rewards or personalized follow-ups.

Conclusion: This week’s data reflects steady overall growth with strong wins in marketing and subscription segments, balanced by actionable insights into areas needing refinement. By addressing checkout friction, optimizing ad spend, and enhancing retention strategies, the business is well-positioned to sustain and accelerate its growth trajectory.
