B. Key Observations

  • New subscriber revenue increased notably versus both prior periods.
  • Conversion rate dropped by 8.6%, indicating potential checkout friction.

III. Detailed Domain Analysis

Revenue & Order Performance

Over the last 14 days, order revenue increased from $435K to $450K (+3.4%), while the number of orders grew by 3.9%. The average order value remained steady at $112.50. Revenue composition shows that new subscriber revenue rose significantly from $190K to $200K.

Subscription Analysis

The subscriber base grew modestly to 15,000 (+2%), with 300 new subscribers. New subscriber revenue increased by 11.1% compared to the previous period and 25% versus last year, while existing subscriber revenue remained stable. The conversion-to-subscription rate stands at 60% with churn at 4.5%.

Marketing Analysis

Marketing spend increased slightly (+2.4%), and overall ROAS improved by 5.0% from 4.0 to 4.2. Efficiency metrics such as MER and CPA show consistent performance, though rising costs indicate the need for closer channel management.

Cohort Analysis

Cohort performance is strong with an average LTV of $120 (up 4.3% vs. P2W) and a repurchase rate of 35%. Supplemental revenue and repeat orders indicate healthy long-term customer value.

IV. Anomaly Detection & Weekly Trend Analysis

  • Conversion Rate: Dropped from 3.5% to 3.2% (–8.6%) in L2W vs. P2W, likely due to checkout friction.
  • CPM Increase: A 20% rise in Paid Social CPM was observed in RCW PY, suggesting intensified competition.
  • New Subscriber Revenue: Up 25% versus L2W PY, highlighting strong acquisition efforts.

V. Success Snapshots

  • Subscriber Acquisition: Record new subscriber revenue was achieved this period.
  • Marketing Efficiency: A creative test improved ROAS and lowered CPA.
  • Overall Stability: Despite minor conversion issues, revenue and order metrics are trending positively.

VI. Recommendations & Action Roadmap

Immediate (Next 7 Days)

  • Optimize the checkout flow to address the conversion rate drop.
  • Refresh creative assets on Paid Search and Paid Social to counter rising CPM/CPC.
  • Investigate any technical issues affecting site performance.

Mid-Term (Next 30 Days)

  • Scale high-performing Paid Social campaigns with refined targeting.
  • Launch targeted retention initiatives to reduce subscriber churn.
  • Further analyze channel performance to optimize marketing spend allocation.

Risks & Open Questions

  • Monitor potential impacts of rising marketing spend on overall ROAS.
  • Conduct A/B testing on checkout variations to identify the root cause of the conversion drop.

VII. Conclusion

Overall, the business demonstrates solid revenue growth and robust subscriber acquisition. Addressing checkout friction and managing rising marketing costs will be essential to sustain this momentum.
